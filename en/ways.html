OUR STORY

INTRODUCTION TIMELINE

Period poverty is a rooted social issue with mainly three causes. We aim to solve it completely.

The first fire: Burn the leaves - economic poverty.

In the Yi Autonomous Region of Sichuan, 189,000 people have a monthly income of less than 1,000RMB, and a year's supply of sanitary napkins costs 600RMB. With only one working person in a household and multiple female members, this is a huge expense for them.

In order to make timely improvements we do so by joining forces with schools across the country to conduct charity bazaars and fundraisers in their respective communities and schools, and donate the funds raised by purchasing sanitary napkins to the local area.

The second fire: Burn the branches - ignorance.

There are no hygiene classes in the schools and girls are only taught how to cope with their menstruation by their female elders. However, in the eyes of the local women elders, leaves, torn newspapers and sand are the most affordable and feasible methods.

So we conducted local talks and lectures on women’s hygiene to educate them. Additionally, we also made brochures about women’s hygiene health and distributed them to girls in remote areas.

The third fire: Burn the roots - shame.

In China, sanitary napkins are purchased in black plastic bags; menstruation is nicknamed and labeled as "bad luck"; accidentally stained pants on the day of menstruation are a sign of impropriety and lead to endless ridicule. Menstruation seems to be a shameful thing. Especially in mountainous areas, the red color of menstruation is a sign of disaster and misfortune due to superstitions there.

Therefore, we are committed to breaking down these stereotypes by creating our own website and social media platforms to post content about women's menstruation and put it into perspective. Through offline activities and distribution of booklets of fighting against stigma, we have been able to talk unabashedly not only from female but also from male about the fact that menstruation is a common and proud thing.

By our actions, we hope no more girls will suffer from the period poverty anymore. Red, a fire, burns the endless poverty.
